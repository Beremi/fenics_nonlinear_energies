\label{sec:numerical-examples}

This section turns from problem definition to computational behaviour. The
benchmark families in \Cref{sec:benchmarks} established the governing
equations, discrete energies, and representative states. Here we focus on
parallel timings, strong scaling, and the comparative cost of alternative
solver or discretisation choices.

\subsection{$p$-Laplace}
\label{sec:num-plaplace}

The $p$-Laplace problem definition and discrete energy are given in
\Cref{sec:bench-plaplace,eq:plaplace-discrete}. In this clean scalar setting,
the numerical story is easiest to interpret: exact element Hessians remain
competitive at distributed scale, while local-SFD stays scientifically aligned
but pays a measurable time premium.

\subsection{Ginzburg--Landau}

stays reliable on a nonconvex scalar energy with the same sparse-infrastructure
