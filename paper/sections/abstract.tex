\begin{abstract}
We present \repo{} as a methods-and-software framework for nonlinear finite-element
solves and optimization that spans \purejax{} references, \fenics{} references where they
exist, and a maintained distributed \jaxpetsc{} implementation. The central
goal is to make exact or near-exact derivative information practical for
difficult nonlinear PDE and PDE-constrained optimization problems while still retaining scalable
nonlinear solvers, sparse linear algebra, and multigrid preconditioning.
Across the maintained benchmark suite we cover nonlinear scalar problems
($p$-Laplace and Ginzburg--Landau), large-deformation hyperelasticity,
two-dimensional and three-dimensional Mohr--Coulomb plasticity, and topology
optimization. The methodology combines automatic differentiation in JAX,
distributed PETSc Newton--Krylov solvers, exact element derivatives and
constitutive
second-order information, and colored sparse finite-difference recovery when
exact Hessians are too expensive. The most demanding maintained study is the
three-dimensional heterogeneous plasticity benchmark on \code{P4(L1\_2)} with
\code{local\_constitutiveAD + local\_pmg}, which reaches the same promoted
nonlinear target with the same reported nonlinear and linear iteration counts
on 1/2/4/8/16/32 MPI ranks and attains $\num{18.05}\times$ end-to-end
speedup at 32 ranks. The topology optimization study complements this with a
distributed design-and-mechanics workflow in which a fine-grid
\jaxpetsc{} implementation remains operational at $768\times 384$ resolution and
32 ranks. We argue that the main contribution is not a single benchmark win,
but an implementation pattern: exact gradients and usable second-order
information can be carried through to distributed nonlinear solves across a
heterogeneous family of FEM problems, including cases in which this
combination is difficult to realize in mainstream high-level PDE toolchains.
\end{abstract}
